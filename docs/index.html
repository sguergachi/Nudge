The Stack

The technology behind Nudge

Harvest Engine

Uses sensor fusion technology to monitor your desktop activity across in real time — silently, locally, privately.

AI Brain

Real-time ML model that ingests Harvest data and predicts focus loss with high confidence before triggering.

Intelligent Alerts

Non-intrusive check-ins that validate predictions. Each answer retrains the model locally — making it smarter every day.

26 Feature Dimensions

Every time Nudge considers checking in, it reads these 26 signals from your desktop and feeds them to the local ML model.

1
Hour of Day
Current hour (0–23). Captures your daily productivity rhythm and when you're naturally most or least focused.
2
Day of Week
Day of the week (0–6). Accounts for weekday vs. weekend patterns in your work habits.
3
Active App
Hashed identifier of the foreground app. Lets Nudge learn which applications you associate with productive work — without storing app names in the cloud.
4
Active Domain
Hashed identifier of the active browser domain. Distinguishes productive research from distracting websites, personalized to your habits.
5
Idle Time
Milliseconds since your last keyboard or mouse input. Detects inactivity, drift, and away-from-keyboard periods.
6
Time in Current App
How long the current app has been continuously in focus. Longer sustained focus is a strong signal of deep work.
7
Title Stability
How long the active window title has been unchanged. A stable title suggests you're engaged with the same content rather than jumping around.
8
App Switches (60s)
Number of app switches in the last 60 seconds. Rapid switching is one of the earliest and strongest signals of distraction.
9
App Switches (5 min)
App switches over the last 5 minutes. Provides a broader window of your switching behavior beyond the immediate moment.
10
Distinct Apps (5 min)
Count of unique apps used in the last 5 minutes. High variety often signals unfocused multitasking.
11
Distinct Domains (5 min)
Unique browser domains visited in the last 5 minutes. Measures the breadth of your web browsing activity.
12
Returned to Primary App
Whether you returned to your main work app within the last 5 minutes. A strong indicator of intentional refocusing after a detour.
13
Current App Share (5 min)
Fraction of the last 5 minutes spent in the current app. A high share means you've been consistently focused on one task.
14
Current Domain Share (5 min)
Fraction of the last 5 minutes on the current browser domain. Same concept as app share, applied to websites.
15
Browser in Focus
Whether a web browser is the foreground app. Activates domain-level signals and adjusts how the model interprets other features.
16
Communication App Active
Whether a messaging or email app is in focus. Short bursts are expected; prolonged stays often indicate distraction.
17
Entertainment Domain
Whether the active site is a known entertainment or distraction domain (e.g. YouTube, Reddit, social media).
18
Work Domain
Whether the active site is a recognized work or productivity domain. Weighted positively by the model.
19
Away from Keyboard
Set when idle time exceeds a threshold. Tells the model you've physically stepped away rather than just pausing to think.
20
Fullscreen Mode
Whether the foreground app is running fullscreen. Correlates with both deep focus (presentations, coding) and passive consumption (video).
21
Workspace Switches (5 min)
Virtual desktop or workspace switches in the last 5 minutes. Frequent changes between workspaces can indicate restlessness.
22
Dev Tool Active
Whether a development tool (IDE, terminal, code editor) is in focus. Typically a strong indicator of a productive context.
23
Creative App Active
Whether a creative application (design tool, video editor, audio workstation) is the foreground app.
24
Office App Active
Whether an office productivity app (document editor, spreadsheet, presentation tool) is in focus.
25
Communication App Category
Category-level flag for communication apps, based on app type rather than individual identity. Complements the specific communication app signal.
26
Entertainment App Active
Whether a known entertainment app (media player, game client) is the foreground application.
