Still focused?

Nudge watches your desktop activity, learns your attention patterns, and asks "still focused?" at exactly the right moment — using a local AI model that runs entirely on your machine.

Download for Windows Download for Linux

Free · Open source · No cloud · No account

Powered by sophisticated technology

Sensor fusion pipeline

The harvest engine samples your foreground app, idle time, and browser domain at 1 Hz. Three raw signals are fused into a 21-dimension feature vector — capturing switch velocity, distinct-app count, and anchor-return events over a rolling 300-second window. Every app is classified into one of six categories at five confidence tiers.

On-device AI model

A scikit-learn binary classifier evaluates each snapshot against your personal YES/NO response history — not a generic population model. Inference runs locally over TCP 127.0.0.1:45002 with sub-100ms latency. Requires just 100 training samples to activate. A background process retrains automatically whenever 50 new responses accumulate.

Adaptive interrupt timing

No fixed schedule. The model interrupts only when it detects drift at ≥98% confidence — otherwise it stays silent or falls back to variable-interval sampling. A single notification appears: YES or NO. Keyboard shortcuts (Ctrl+Shift+Y / Ctrl+Shift+N) keep you in flow. Every answer sharpens the next prediction.

What sets Nudge apart

Your model, not someone else's

Trained exclusively on your responses. No pre-trained population model. It knows what distraction looks like for you.

Zero data leaves your machine

All training data, model weights, and config stay in ~/.nudge/. No cloud endpoints exist in the codebase. No telemetry, no accounts, no signup.

Every response retrains the model

Every YES or NO you give becomes a training sample. The model retrains automatically — getting sharper with every interaction.

Keyboards stay in flow

Ctrl+Shift+Y for YES, Ctrl+Shift+N for NO. The notification auto-dismisses after 30 seconds. Your hands never leave the keyboard.

Cross-platform

Windows 10/11 and Linux — KDE, GNOME, Sway, Cinnamon. Wayland and X11. Self-contained binary, no runtime install required.

Open source (MIT)

Every line is on GitHub. Auditable, forkable, improvable. Free forever — no subscription, no premium tier.
