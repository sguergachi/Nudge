How it works

01

Runs in your system tray

Nudge sits quietly in your notification area. It samples your foreground app, idle time, and browser domain once per second. No screenshots. No keystrokes. No network.

02

Learns your patterns

Every app is sorted into a category — Development, Creative, Office, Communication, Entertainment, Utility. A local AI compares each moment against your past YES/NO responses. The more you use it, the smarter it gets.

03

Asks one question

A small window appears: "Still focused?" YES or NO. Keyboard shortcuts so you never leave your flow. Auto-dismisses after 30 seconds. Every answer trains the model further.

Everything you need, nothing you don't

Adaptive timing

Nudge doesn't use fixed timers. It waits until your attention pattern shifts — then asks. Sometimes that's 5 minutes, sometimes 40. It adjusts to how you actually work.

On-device AI

No cloud dependency. A scikit-learn classifier trains locally on your responses and runs inferences over TCP. Your data never leaves ~/.nudge/.

Keyboard-friendly

Ctrl+Shift+Y for YES, Ctrl+Shift+N for NO. The notification auto-dismisses after 30 seconds. You never have to touch the mouse.

Live analytics

Track your focus trends over time. See daily, weekly, and monthly breakdowns of what you classified as productive vs. not — all from your system tray.

Your data stays yours

No accounts. No telemetry. No network calls from the daemon. Everything lives in ~/.nudge/ and never leaves. Open source, MIT licensed.

Cross-platform

Windows 10/11 and Linux (KDE, GNOME, Sway, Cinnamon). Works on both Wayland and X11. Self-contained binaries — no runtime installation needed.

Under the hood

A four-stage pipeline. Every piece runs locally.

Signal acquisition
Foreground app, idle duration, window title, and compositor metadata sampled at 1 Hz. Browser domain extracted from window title when a browser is focused.
Source: KWin Script / X11 EWMH / Sway IPC / Win32 Quality: Trusted · Usable · Poor
Classification
Every app and domain tagged as Development, Creative, Office, Communication, Entertainment, or Utility at one of five confidence tiers. A rolling 300 s window tracks switch frequency, app diversity, and anchor-return events.
Output: 21-dimensional feature vector Window: 300 s
Inference
21-feature snapshot evaluated against a scikit-learn classifier served over TCP 127.0.0.1:45002. Requires 100 training samples; retrains at 50+ new samples via 300 s polling.
Threshold: ≥98% confidence Check interval: 60 s
Interrupt
Custom Avalonia overlay with a binary choice: YES or NO. Keyboard shortcuts (Ctrl+Shift+Y / Ctrl+Shift+N). 30 s auto-dismiss. Every response appended to the training set.
IPC: UDP 127.0.0.1:45001 Model: scikit-learn (Python)
