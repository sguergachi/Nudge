Harvest Engine

What's running on your machine. In real time.

The Nudge Harvest Engine is a sensor fusion system that continuously observes three things: which application is in focus, whether you're idle, and what domain you're on if you're in a browser. Every observation is graded for reliability — Trusted when it comes from a direct compositor API, Usable when the category is uncertain, and Poor when it had to fall back to a heuristic scan. This quality score tells the AI exactly how much weight to give each reading.

Raw signals flow into a rolling window that tracks your behaviour over the last several minutes: how often you switch apps, how many distinct programs you've used, what percentage of time you've spent in each one, and how often you return to your primary tool (your anchor app). These behavioural patterns are compressed into 21 features — a concise numeric fingerprint of your current focus state that the AI evaluates every 60 seconds.

  • Three raw signals: app focus, idle time, and browser domain — nothing else
  • Every reading graded Trusted / Usable / Poor so the model weighs it correctly
  • 21-feature fingerprint extracted from rolling behavioural patterns
Smart Categorisation

It knows the difference between coding and scrolling.

Every application and browser domain Nudge observes is automatically classified into one of six categories: Development, Creative & Design, Office & Writing, Communication, Entertainment, or Utility. The classification uses multiple confidence tiers — Override (user-defined), Desktop (OS ground truth from the installed application metadata), Semantic (keyword matching in the app name or ID), Inferred (browser tabs inherit the anchor app's category), and Fallback (defaults to Utility when nothing matches).

This category system is how Nudge understands context. An hour in VS Code categorised as Development reads differently from an hour in Slack categorised as Communication. When the AI sees you spending time in Entertainment apps mid-morning, that signal — combined with your switch velocity and idle ratio — pushes the prediction toward "drifting." The categories give the model the contextual awareness to distinguish deep work from genuine distraction, tailored to how you actually use your machine.

  • Six categories: Development, Creative, Office, Communication, Entertainment, Utility
  • Five-tier confidence: Override > Desktop > Semantic > Inferred > Fallback
  • Browser tabs inherit anchor app category — no misclassification when you're working
AI Brain

Learns you. Not people like you.

The AI Brain is a model built exclusively from your YES/NO answers — it has never seen another person's data. Every check-in feeds your personal model: it maps the current feature vector (your app category, idle ratio, switch velocity, anchor return behaviour, browser domain signals, and 14 other temporal features) against whether you responded focused or drifting. Over time it learns that a morning in VS Code with low switch frequency and no idle time means you're locked in, while rapid switches through Communication and Entertainment apps mid-afternoon is a reliable drift signature.

When the 21-feature vector is evaluated, the Brain produces a confidence score. If that score crosses a high conviction threshold, it triggers a check-in. Below that threshold it stays silent — even if you're technically in an Entertainment app, the model accounts for context (maybe it's your lunch break, or you often work with music playing). This means the Brain adapts to your actual workflow rather than applying rigid rules. It won't bother you when you're deep in flow, but it will catch the moments when you've genuinely lost the plot.

  • Personal model — your data never mixes with anyone else's
  • Combines category, switch velocity, idle ratio, anchor return, and domain signals
  • High conviction threshold — no false alarms, no interruptions during flow
Full control

A gentle nudge when it matters. Full control when you need it.

When the AI Brain detects you're drifting, a small check-in window appears with a simple question: still focused? Two buttons — YES and NO. Keyboard shortcuts let you answer without touching the mouse. If you don't respond, the window quietly dismisses itself. The settings panel gives you full command: turn the AI on or off, set how often you want to be checked, manage sound preferences, and see how the model is doing — how many samples it's collected and how accurate it's become at reading your patterns.

  • Fades in when it matters, dismisses silently if you're busy
  • Answer with a click or a keyboard shortcut — your choice
  • Toggle AI, adjust check frequency, monitor model accuracy — all from one panel
How it works

Sense → Fuse → Predict → Nudge

A four-stage pipeline that runs entirely on your machine — from raw desktop signals to a split-second YES/NO check-in. No cloud, no telemetry, no pointless fixed timers.

1

Sense

The Harvest Engine continuously observes what's on your screen — which application is active, how long you've been there, whether you've stepped away, and what website you're on if you're in a browser. Every observation is tagged with a confidence score so the system knows how reliable it is. No screenshots, no recordings, no keystroke logs — just the name of what's in focus.

2

Classify

Every app and browser domain is automatically tagged with a category — Development, Creative, Office, Communication, Entertainment, or Utility — using a five-tier confidence system. The OS metadata is the most reliable signal, followed by keyword patterns in the app name, then inference from your anchor app if you're in a browser. This means Nudge knows the difference between you coding in VS Code (Development), answering a message in Slack (Communication), and watching YouTube (Entertainment), without you having to configure anything.

3

Fuse & Predict

The category, idle ratio, switch frequency, anchor return behaviour, and domain signals are compressed into a 21-feature fingerprint of your current focus state. The AI Brain evaluates this fingerprint against everything it has learned from your previous YES/NO responses. If the pattern matches one it has seen before when you said you were drifting, and conviction is high, it signals a check-in. If it sees a productive rhythm — even in a category that might look distracting out of context — it stays silent.

4

Nudge

A small, unobtrusive window appears and asks a simple question: still focused? YES keeps you moving, NO logs the drift. If you're in the middle of something, keyboard shortcuts let you answer without leaving your flow. Leave it unanswered and it quietly disappears on its own. Every response feeds back into the model, making the next prediction sharper. Over time, Nudge learns exactly when to speak up and when to stay silent.
