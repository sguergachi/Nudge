How it works

01

Watches your desktop

Sits quietly in your system tray. Samples your foreground app, idle time, and browser domain once per second. No screenshots. No keystrokes. No network calls.

02

Learns your patterns

Every app is classified into a category — Development, Creative, Office, Communication, Entertainment, Utility. A local AI model compares each moment against your past YES/NO responses and gets sharper with every answer.

03

Asks one question

A small window appears with two buttons: YES or NO. Keyboard shortcuts so you never leave your flow. Auto-dismisses after 30 seconds. Every answer trains the model further.

Features

Adaptive timing

No fixed timers. Nudge waits until your attention pattern shifts — then asks. Sometimes that's 5 minutes, sometimes 40. It adjusts to how you actually work.

On-device AI

A scikit-learn classifier trains locally on your responses and runs inference over TCP. Your data never leaves ~/.nudge/. No cloud dependency.

Keyboard-first

Ctrl+Shift+Y for YES, Ctrl+Shift+N for NO. The notification auto-dismisses. You never have to touch the mouse.

Live analytics

Track your focus trends over time from the system tray. Daily, weekly, and monthly breakdowns of what you classified as productive vs. not.

Your data stays yours

No accounts. No telemetry. No network calls from the daemon. Everything lives in ~/.nudge/ and never leaves. MIT licensed.

Cross-platform

Windows 10/11 and Linux (KDE, GNOME, Sway, Cinnamon). Wayland and X11. Self-contained binaries — no runtime required.

Under the hood

A four-stage pipeline. Every piece runs locally.

Signal acquisition

Foreground app, idle duration, window title, and compositor metadata at 1 Hz. Browser domain extracted from window title.

KWin Script · X11 EWMH · Sway IPC · Win32
Classification

Every app and domain tagged into 6 categories at 5 confidence tiers. A rolling 300 s window tracks switches, diversity, and anchor-return events.

21-feature vector · 300 s rolling window
Inference

Snapshot evaluated against a scikit-learn classifier on TCP 127.0.0.1:45002. Requires 100 training samples; retrains at 50+ new samples.

≥98% confidence threshold · 60 s check interval
Interrupt

Custom Avalonia overlay: YES or NO. Ctrl+Shift+Y / Ctrl+Shift+N. 30 s auto-dismiss. Every response trains the model.

UDP 127.0.0.1:45001 · scikit-learn (Python)
