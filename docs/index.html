How it works

01

Sensor fusion pipeline

Three raw signals — foreground app, idle duration, and browser domain — are sampled at 1 Hz. A rolling 300 s window captures switch velocity, distinct-app count, and anchor-return events. Every app is classified into one of six categories at five confidence tiers, from OS ground truth down to sensible defaults.

Sources: KWin Script · X11 EWMH · Sway IPC · Win32 · domain extraction
02

On-device AI model

A scikit-learn binary classifier evaluates each 26-dimension snapshot against your personal YES/NO response history. Not a generic population model — your model, trained on your data. Inference over TCP 127.0.0.1:45002 at sub-100ms latency. Activates at 100 samples; retrains at every 50 new responses.

RandomForest / LogisticRegression · sub-100ms · retrains automatically
03

Adaptive interrupt timing

No fixed schedule — the model decides. Fires a notification only when drift confidence reaches ≥98%. Stays silent when you're productive. Below threshold: falls back to variable-interval sampling at 5–10 minutes. Ctrl+Shift+Y / Ctrl+Shift+N respond without leaving the keyboard. Every answer sharpens the next prediction.

30s auto-dismiss · keyboard shortcuts · UDP 127.0.0.1:45001

What sets Nudge apart

Trained on you, not a population

No pre-trained model. Every response you give becomes a training sample. Your model learns your distraction fingerprint — and gets sharper with every interaction.

Zero data leaves your machine

No cloud endpoints exist in the codebase. Training data, model weights, and config stay in ~/.nudge/. No telemetry, no accounts, no signup.

One question, one decision

"Still focused?" — YES or NO. No complex UI, no settings maze. The model watches; you respond. That's the entire interaction loop.

No fixed timers

Not a Pomodoro. Not a schedule. The model interrupts only when it's confident you're drifting. Sometimes that's 5 minutes, sometimes 40. It adapts.

Cross-platform

Windows 10/11 and Linux (KDE, GNOME, Sway, Cinnamon). Wayland + X11. Self-contained binary — no runtime install required.

Open source (MIT)

Every line on GitHub. Auditable, forkable, improvable. Free forever — no subscription, no premium tier.
